Dashboard


The dataset used for this project can be found at Kaggle here.

We will explore the data to determine which factors correlate with an increased cost.

A heatmap of the data shows the strongest correlation between smoking and charges.

There is also a lesser correlation between BMI and age to charges. We will plot these factors vs. the charges below.


First we will plot a histogram to show the frequency of charges in different price buckets.


As shown in the histogram, most charges are less than $15,000, with the highest frequency of charges being around ~$5,000 or less.


The scatter plot of smoker vs. charges shows how smokers are always charged greater than $10,000. Whereas we learned in the previous histogram that the highest frequency of charges are actually less than $5,000. This shows a huge cost increase for smokers.


Some correlation can also be seen of increased charges with an increase in age.


Less of a correlation is apparent between BMI and charges.
